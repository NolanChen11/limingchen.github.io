Crowdsourcing Reading Aids For Narrative Text

-Yuchen Wang, Jinhan Wu, Liming Chen.

Design Document


In this project, I worked with two group mates and co-designed a workflow that uses crowdsourcing to generate understandable graphic reading aid for each natural paragraph from narrative text.

We noticed the linguistic and technological barriers around the world that prevent low literacy people from accessing key information. Thus, our goal is to build a cohesive system of human and computer to enhance global communication by helping low literacy people understand narrative text better at a low cost. At the same time, the system allows everyone, including those who don't have graphic desgin skills, to create graphic reading aids by summerizing, keywords marking, image collecting and collaging.

Stakeholders

Our research shows who are our stakeholders and their limitations when reading:

  • Low literacy
  • Multiple spoken dialects & Multiple written languages
  • Technological Barrier (no access to the internet, shared smartphones)


Test & Interview

We tested our prototype on our cohorts. In this phase, we aimed to figure out the practicality of results generated by the workflow and the challenges encountered by workers. After the Test, we collected feedbacks from workers:

  • “It is difficult to summarize two summarized sentences.”
  • ““this is a complex task, and we need to get more paid.”
  • “searching images by the keyword constrains creativity. It would be fun if I was given the entire paragraph.”
  • “It is so hard to find a proper image for verbs.”
  • “should indicate how to search for images, for example, a link to an image set.”
  • “PhotoShop is hard to use.”
  • ......

Workflow Overview

The final workflow collaboratively uses computer algorithms and human computation tasks to generate visual representations for each paragraph from narrative text.

  1. Split the narrative text into natural paragraphs.
  2. Summarize every paragraph.
  3. Mark Keywords from the summarization.
  4. For each summarized paragraph, collect images by keywords from the summarization and save them to an image set.
  5. Collaging the images into visual representations for each paragraph with the consistency of image selection.


Results

Since we don't have the opportunity and budget to deploy this workflow to recruited workers on AMT, we simulate the results by assigning these tasks to the people around us. We used Google Docs as the platform for writing tasks (summarization, marking keywords) and Google Drive as the image set where people could save the images they collected. For the collaging task, we chose to use Photoshop to operate images
